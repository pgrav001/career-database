# Stories — STAR-format behavioral narratives

> _One file per behavioral interview story you can tell. Bound to evidence; structured for spoken delivery._

## What goes here

A "story" is a behavioral-interview-ready narrative — usually 2–5 minutes spoken. It typically maps to one or more standard prompts ("Tell me about a time you led through a crisis", "Tell me about a time you disagreed with leadership", etc.).

Each story binds claims (from `[[strengths]]`) to proof (from `[[evidence]]`) in narrative form.

## Frontmatter

```yaml
---
themes: [leadership, resilience, conflict, ...]    # what prompts this answers
role:                                              # which role it happened in
duration: ~2min | ~5min                            # spoken length
primary_evidence: [[evidence/...]]                 # the quantified proof
---
```

## STAR structure

Each story file uses STAR:
- **S**ituation — what was the context
- **T**ask — what was specifically your responsibility
- **A**ction — what you did (decision points, judgment calls, trade-offs you named)
- **R**esult — what happened, ideally with `[[evidence/...]]` linked metric

Plus:
- **Reflection** — what you learned, what you'd do differently. *Always include this.*
- **Variants** — how to compress to 2 min / expand to 5 min / pivot if the interviewer asks a follow-up.

## Naming

`{theme-or-event}.md` — examples:
- `layoff-management.md`
- `disagreed-with-leadership.md`
- `bet-against-skepticism.md`
- `time-i-failed.md`

## Standard behavioral prompts to cover

Most senior loops will draw from this set. Use this as a coverage checklist:

- A time you led through a crisis / RIF / major reorg
- A time you disagreed with leadership and had to act anyway
- A time you failed
- A time you championed something through skepticism
- A time you managed someone out (if applicable)
- A hiring mistake
- A time you didn't get something you wanted (promotion, budget, scope)
- A time you grew someone significantly
- A time you had to deliver hard feedback
- A time you took a strategic risk that paid off
- A time you took a strategic risk that didn't pay off
- A time you had to influence without authority
- A time you had to make a decision with incomplete information

The Tier 2 OPEN_QUESTIONS items often map directly to gaps in this list.
