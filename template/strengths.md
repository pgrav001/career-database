# Signature Strengths

> _The durable patterns of how you operate — visible across every cycle of your career, not just any single project._

## What this is (and isn't)

**Strengths are the claims.** Your job-search narrative leads with how you're effective — and proves it with examples. This file is the claim layer.

| Layer | What | Where |
|---|---|---|
| **The claims** (how I operate) | Durable behavioral patterns | `strengths.md` (this file) + `themes/*.md` deep-dives |
| **The proof** (what I did) | Quantified accomplishments | `evidence/*.md` |
| **The proof in story form** | STAR-format narratives | `stories/*.md` |
| **The third-party proof** | Peer + manager quotes | `voice/peer-quotes.md` |

Strengths are the **headline material** for resume summaries, LinkedIn About sections, elevator pitches, and cover letter openers. They're what make you distinctive vs. interchangeable. Each strength below should link to a deep-dive in `themes/` and to the supporting evidence + story files.

When in doubt: **lead with the claim, prove with the example, in your voice.**

## Picking your 6 strengths

Aim for 6 (± 1). Each strength must:
- Show up across multiple distinct roles or cycles (not just one)
- Have at least 2 quantifiable examples backing it
- Have at least 1 third-party quote validating it
- Be recognizably *you*, not a generic professional virtue

**Don't fill this file from the top down.** Strengths emerge from substance. Mine your evidence + stories + peer quotes first; the strengths reveal themselves as patterns. Filling this top-down produces generic claims that read as generic.

Once you have candidates, pressure-test:
- Are these *the* 6, or are we missing one / merging two?
- Are any feeling forced?
- Does someone who knows you well recognize you in this list?

## How to use this file

- **Resume summary:** pick 2–3 strengths that match the target role and weave them into the opening paragraph
- **LinkedIn About:** lead with 1 strength + 1 supporting accomplishment as proof
- **Elevator pitch (30s):** name the strength, give the proof, name what you want next
- **Cover letter opener:** frame around the strength most relevant to the company's stated challenge
- **Interview ("what makes you distinctive"):** any single strength is a 60-second answer; any pair is a 2-minute answer

## Open recall

- Which strengths feel most *true* to you? Which feel like a stretch?
- Which strengths are you most/least comfortable claiming publicly?
- Are there strengths missing from this list? Candidates to evaluate?
- Are there any of these six that should be merged or split?
